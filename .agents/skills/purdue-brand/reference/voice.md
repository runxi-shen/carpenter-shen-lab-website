# Purdue Voice & Tone

Source: marcom.purdue.edu/our-brand/voice-tone (public, fetched 2026-05-27).

## Six brand attributes

Every piece of Purdue-attributed copy should embody these. Use them as filters on headlines, hero text, calls to action, and section intros.

1. **Driven** — "relentless in everything they do. We never give up." Use action verbs; avoid hedging language.
2. **Balanced** — STEM, arts, and athletics are equally valued. Don't elevate one discipline by belittling another.
3. **Intentional** — "focused and deliberate in our actions." Specifics beat vagueness; cut filler.
4. **Innovative** — exploring new approaches. Show how, don't just claim novelty.
5. **Inclusive** — "a collection of exceptional people." Use "we" generously; credit collaborators.
6. **Respected** — "credible, reliable and rigorous." Back claims with evidence; avoid hype.

## Foundational message

> **"The Persistent Pursuit of the Next Giant Leap"**
>
> Inspired by Neil Armstrong (Purdue alum, 1955). Forward-looking, collaborative ("no one takes a giant leap all alone"), encompasses all disciplines.

## Secondary language

- **"Again and Again and Again"** — tireless effort.
- **"We Keep Going"** — unwavering drive.

These are official Purdue messaging hooks. Use sparingly — once in a hero, never twice on the same page.

## Lab-specific application

The Carpenter-Shen lab's mission statement on the homepage —

> *"Very few people get to solve puzzles all day long and directly help patients suffering from disease."*

— already lands well against **Driven**, **Intentional**, and **Inclusive** (it credits the broader privilege of doing the work). It's compatible with the Purdue voice; don't rewrite it to force the "giant leap" frame.

## Writing-style rules (compounded with CLAUDE.md)

From this repo's CLAUDE.md, kept here so the skill is self-contained:

- **No em dashes** in body text. Use commas or restructure.
- Body paragraphs are **justified** (both sides aligned).
- Section eyebrows use the `.section-label` class (Barlow Condensed, UPPERCASE, gold).
- Section subtitles under headers are `text-center` (override the global justify).

Compatible with Purdue voice — keep all four.

## Do / don't

- **Do** lead with concrete claims ("we develop CellProfiler, used by 100k researchers") not vague ones ("we are committed to excellence").
- **Do** name collaborators (the lab is "Carpenter-Shen", not just "Carpenter"); models the *Inclusive* attribute.
- **Do** acknowledge persistence — biology is hard, results take years. Aligns with *Driven*.
- **Don't** use marketing superlatives ("world-class", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class"). Erodes *Respected*.
- **Don't** stack adjectives. Pick one. Earn it.
- **Don't** use the "giant leap" frame in every section. It's a seasoning, not a base.
